Palm Oil

Palm Oil is an oil extracted from the fruit of the trees called African Oil Palms. It is used in food products, soap and biodiesel. Palm Oil's high consumption and production has been at the center of multiple controversies in Europe. We will study the extent of Palm Oil use is food products in European and North American countries and will try to understand where Palm Oil's bad reputation comes from.

How common is Palm Oil in European food products?

European food products containing Palm Oil 3.43%.

Is it bad for your health?

Palm oil is not particularly dangerous for your health. Indeed, its consumption is not more dangerous than many other edible oils.

However, as we can see in the boxplot, food products containing Palm Oil receive on average worse nutritional grades. As seen above, this is mainly due to Palm Oil being used in "junk food" categories such as Salty and Sugary Snacks.

Why does palm oil have a bad reputation if it is so common?

Where is it produced? Economic situation of countries / regions where it is produced

As we see in the map on the right, Palm Oil Palm oil plantations and Mills are mosltly in South East Asia with some in Central and South America. The countries from these regions are less economically developed nations compared to European and North American countries.

For example, Indonesia has more than 80% of the world's Palm Oil Mills but also has a GDP per capita 20X smaller than Switzerland's

Consequences

Palm Oil is produced in countries which are less economically developed than the countries where the products containing it are sold. This often means the the main producers (Indonesia, Myanmar, ...) have less strict and enforced Environmental Policies than western countries. This leads Palm Oil producing Companies to expand their plantations without control. The huge demand for Palm Oil in food products is the cause of a rapid expansion in Palm Oil Plantation's land use. This is the reason why Palm Oil is blamed for:

  • deforestation
  • Habitat destruction (Orangutans, Tigers, Elephants)
  • Air and Water Pollution
    • Learn More

    Solutions

    As we saw early, many well know Western and Swiss companies (Nestle, Kellogs, Lindt, ...) massively use Palm Oil in their food products. Consumers should pressure these companies into choosing environmentally friendly palm oil producers which follow strict guidelines about producing palm oil sustainably.

    Comparing sugar in Swiss and North American products

    In this part we compare the quantity of sugar available in Swiss and North American products. We find why Switzerland has a more important offering of sugary snacks than North America.

    Is sugar relly bad?

    It depends a lot. The sugar contained in fruits is very different from the sugar contained in soft drinks.
    A simplification is to define free sugar as the bad kind of sugar. The World Health Organisation suggests in a recent article not to exceed ~50g of Free sugars per day; if possible less than ~25g.

    Overall on all products, who offers the most sugary food?

    The median sugar of manufactured items in the US and Canada is 20% higher than in any European country.

    However, according to a 2015 study , sugar consumption in the US is ~68% higher than in Switzerland. Before explaining that, we need to see where all this sugar come from.

    class="spotlight style1 orient-right content-align-left image-position-center onscroll-image-fade-in" style="background-color:white">

    What kind of products contains the most added sugar?

    Sugary snacks (biscuits, candies, chocolate) contains (in average in Europe and North America) 35% of sugars. In average we should not consume more than 140g of sugary snacks, according to the WHO.

    Is the sugar distribution similar in US and Swizerland?

    It appears that our snacks are not better than in northern America: actually they contain more sugar!

    However, our groceries items (important examples include bread, breakfast cereals, tomato sauce, meats) contains much less sugar.

    This is an important observation, which could explain the difference of sugar consumption between the US and Switzerland: not really because of the 'junk food'; mainly because their groceries contains more added sugar.

    Why are our snacks so sugary?

    To understand that, let's first take a look at the brands with the highest amount different products, , and see which brands uses sugar the most.

    As we can see, Nestle is the big winner, as it sells a huge amount of chocolate and other sugary products. An average milk chocolate contains 50% of sugar, much more than the average sugary snack. And Nestle is from Switzerland!

    Conclusion

    In terms of sugar, the problem is quite different in Swizerland and in northern America.

    Switzerland is the world biggest chocolate consumer, according to Wikipedia or Forbes. In Swizerland, there is a some correlation between sugary food chocolate, and Nestle. The keywords of Nestle products is quite close to the keywords of sugary snacks in Swizerland: mostly chocolate!

    In Northern America, the sugary snacks are more various: examples include popcorn, biscuits, and candies. Surprisingly, popcorn is the most common sweetened product! But in average those snacks contains less sugar than chocolate.

    From the perspective of the openfoodfacts dataset, sugary snacks is probably not the reason why people in the US consume more sugar. The main reason could be that most of their casual groceries contains more sugar.

    Where do we have the greatest variety of additives?

    France and the US are the only two countries with more than 300 possible additives in their entries. What happens when countries are ranked by the proportion of dangerous additives?

    Interestingly, France has a very low rate of dangerous additives whereas the US is by far the country where these additives are the most present.
